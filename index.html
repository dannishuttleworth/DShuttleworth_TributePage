Sir David Frederick Attenborough

Nature's voice and the UK's National Treasure

David Attenborough has a career that spans over 50 years. Although many people recognise him for his beautiful narrations on wildlife documentaries, he spent much of his life both in front and behind the camera.
Below outlines some of his career achievements and movements, but is by no means exhaustive.

  • Trainee Producer (1952)
    David Attenborough's career started when he joined the bbc in 1952 as a traineer producer. Here, he achieve his first television credit on the short film Coelacanth.
  • BBC Zoo Quest Producer and Presenter (1954-1961)
    During his time producing BBC Zoo Quest David had to step in as presenter when illness struck the film camp. It was the first time viewers saw him in front of the camera interacting with animals, often in exotic and remote locations.
  • Forms the BBC's Travel and Exploration Unit (1957)
    After declining to join the BBC Natural History Unit due to location, David set up his own department, the Travel and Exploration Unit. He continued to present Zoo Quest as well as produce other documentaries.
  • BBC 2 Controller (1965)
    In 1965 David took on the creative and managerial responsibility for running BBC 2. But he made sure his contract terms allowed him some flexbility to continue to write and produce.
  • BBC Director of Programmes (1969)
    When David became the BBC's Director of Programmes he became responsible for the output of both BBC channels. His tasks included budgeting, attending board meetings and managing staff. All far removed from the business of filming and writing programmes.
  • Freelance Broadcaster (1972-present)
    David resigned from his position within the BBC and moved back into producing and writing, this time as a freelance broadcaster. He subsequently wrote, produced and narrated world-renowned nature documentaries including Life on Earth (1979), The Living Planet (1984), Life in the Freezer (1993), The Life of Birds (1998), The Blue Planet (2001) and Are We Changing Planet Earth? (2006).
    In his later documentaries he starting raising awareness on environmenal issues, including climate change.
  • Documentary Narrator and Environmentalist(2000-present)
    David Attenborough continues to narrate nature documentaries to date. From 2006 he became more vocal in the public eye regarding the impact of humans on the natural world, especially our influence on climate change. In his 2020 documentary film David Attenborough: A Life on Our Planet , he gave his first-hand views and concerns for the current state of the planet. But alongside his concerns, he also shares his hopes.
